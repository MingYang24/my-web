Programme note:

The installation originates from a mind travel between two friends, one from Beijing and the other from Tripoli. Despite never having physically visited each other's cities, their communication serves as a channel for exploration of each other's cultures. The piece expresses the notion of home and the dreams experience moments of connection, understanding, miscommunication and loss.

A variety of symbols are employed to represent the diverse encounters one may come across during their travels. Marbles depict individual journeys. As they roll and move, they symbolize the paths taken by each person as they navigate through life. The presence of a rolypoly base influences the movement of these marbles, adding an element of unpredictability to their trajectories. This unpredictability reflects the ever-changing nature of travel and highlights the need for adaptability to new circumstances. Furthermore, it emphasizes the qualities of resilience and flexibility required to successfully navigate through unfamiliar territories.
